Agridera Fresh Market
Banana Varieties

*****

Agridera aims to be one of the world's leading companies in the breeding, production, and marketing of banana plants. We invest intensively in research and development to provide solutions to growers and consumers in numerous tropical and subtropical countries around the world.
In particular, we breed improved disease-resistant cultivars adapted to different climates and markets globally.

Currently, emphasis is placed on combining resistance to Panama Disease (Fusarium wilt TR4) and Black Sigatoka with other soil-borne challenges into superior banana breeding lines. Moreover, comprehensive efforts are being undertaken to develop varieties resistant to Banana Bunchy Top Virus (BBTV).

In terms of horticultural traits, breeding is for yield, different bunch sizes, cold tolerance, flavor, post-harvest longevity, and wind resistance. Combinations of quality features and disease resistance are sought in a variety of types, namely sweet dessert bananas and starchy plantains.

Agridera Banana breeding programs are focusing on three main segments: Commercial Cavendish, Cooking Bananas (Plantains), and Specialty Bananas.

Commercial Cavendish Program

This breeding is aimed for the global export markets. The main focus is on high yield, uniform bunch shape, and robust resistance to Panama disease (Race 1 and TR4). These hybrids are developed to ensure long shelf life and perfect bright yellow color upon ripening.

Cooking Bananas & Plantains

This breeding is for African, Latin American, and Caribbean markets. We focus on large, starchy fruits suited for frying, boiling, or baking. Emphasis is placed on drought tolerance, vigorous growth, and high resistance to Black Weevil and nematodes.

Specialty & Cold Tolerant
Bananas

This breeding is for niche markets and subtropical climates. We breed unique flavor profiles, such as vanilla or berry notes, and distinct skin colors (red, blue/silver). We combine robust root systems with cold and wind tolerance to allow cultivation outside traditional tropical zones.

Agridera operates through international and local distributers around the world.
